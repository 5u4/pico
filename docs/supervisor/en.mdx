The supervisor (`crates/supervisor/`) is a small Rust daemon that owns the pico
**worker process's lifecycle**: it spawns `bun run src/index.ts`, blocks until
the worker reports ready, and hot-swaps between deploy slots for zero-downtime
updates and rollback. The TypeScript side of the handshake is `ready.ts`; the
[boot sequence](carto:overview) calls it near the end.

## Two processes, one handshake

There are two programs: the Rust **supervisor** daemon and the TypeScript
**worker** it manages. They speak newline-delimited JSON over a Unix socket at
`~/.pico/supervisor/pico.sock`.

- The supervisor manages **exactly one worker at a time**
  (`AsyncMutex<Option<WorkerProc>>`, `crates/supervisor/src/supervisor.rs:37-51`).
- The same socket serves an operator CLI (`client.rs`: `deploy`, `rollback`,
  `status`, `stop`) and the worker's ready ping.

## The wire protocol

`proto.rs` defines it (`crates/supervisor/src/proto.rs`):

- `Request` (tag `cmd`): `Deploy{path}`, `Rollback`, `Status`, `Stop`,
  `Ready{token}` (`crates/supervisor/src/proto.rs:7-14`).
- `Response` (tag `status`): `Ok{detail}`, `Status(StatusReport)`, `Error{message}`
  (`crates/supervisor/src/proto.rs:17-22`).
- `ReadyAck{}` serializes to `{}` — the exact ack `ready.ts` awaits
  (`crates/supervisor/src/proto.rs:25`).

## The ready handshake

This is the join between the two processes.

**Worker side** — `reportReady(socketPath, token)`
(`src/supervisor/ready.ts:7-35`): connects to the socket, and on connect writes
a single frame `{"cmd":"ready","token":...}\n` (`src/supervisor/ready.ts:26`). It
resolves `ok()` when the supervisor's ack `data` arrives, or errors on timeout
(`READY_TIMEOUT_MS = 10s`), socket error, or a close before ack.

**Supervisor side** — when the worker is spawned, `spawn_and_validate`
(`crates/supervisor/src/supervisor.rs`) stores a `oneshot` sender keyed by a
freshly minted token in `pending_ready`, then launches `bun run src/index.ts`
with env `NODE_ENV=production`, `PICO_SUPERVISOR_SOCKET=<socket>`, and
`PICO_READY_TOKEN=<token>`. It then `select`s on: the ready oneshot firing, the
child exiting early, or a `health_timeout` (default 30s) elapsing. `handle_conn`
routes an inbound `Ready` to `signal_ready`, which matches the token against
`pending_ready`, fires the oneshot (unblocking the spawn), and writes `ReadyAck`
(`crates/supervisor/src/supervisor.rs:197-206`). A worker that never acks in time
is killed and the deploy fails.

## Blue/green slots

`slots.rs` implements deploy/rollback as **symlink swaps** under `<dir>/slots`
(`crates/supervisor/src/slots.rs`):

- `current` and `previous` symlinks point at slot directories; `current_target()`
  / `previous_target()` read them (`:17-23`).
- `promote(slot)` moves `current → previous` and points `current` at the new slot
  (`:25-30`); `swap()` exchanges the two for rollback (`:32-42`).
- `set_link` writes a `.tmp` symlink then renames over the target so the swap is
  **atomic** (`:53-62`).

A deploy validates the target slot contains `src/index.ts`, kills the old
worker, and `spawn_and_validate`s the new one; on success it `promote`s, on
failure it `recover`s to the previous slot. Only two slots are ever kept.

## Configuration & shutdown

`config.rs` reads `~/.pico/supervisor/supervisor.toml` (all-default if absent):
`socket_path`, `bun_path`, and `health_timeout_secs` (default 30). `resolve_bun`
searches `$BUN_INSTALL/bin/bun`, `$HOME/.bun/bin/bun`, `PATH`, then bare `bun`
(`crates/supervisor/src/config.rs:66-81`).

On `SIGTERM`/`SIGINT` the supervisor cancels its task tracker, drains
connections, and SIGTERMs the worker; the worker independently catches the same
signals and runs its own [graceful shutdown](carto:overview) — the two shutdown
paths are separate and both required.
